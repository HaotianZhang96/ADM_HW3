As a child he had a keen interest in spiritual things, reading spiritual books from the library and creating his own esoteric ceremony at eight years old. He experienced many supernatural phenomena including ESP and poltergeist activity in his family home.<br /><br />In the early 80s he extensively studied the works of Jiddu Krishnamurti, seeing him twice in person in both 1982 and 1983 at Brockwood Park in the UK. He also read many other spiritual books and looked into other spiritual groups and traditions around this time.<br /><br />In 1990 Belzebuub stated he came across a set of spiritual practices that he found very effective, and would go on to teach.<br /><br />Belzebuub would later author online courses that proved very popular and grew to be the largest of their kind in the world at the time. These courses were run online and in person in eight countries across several continents until 2010. This course content also formed the basis of Belzebuub’s books. <br /><br />BELZEBUUB’S BOOKS AND OTHER WORKS<br /><br />Belzebuub’s work contained information on how to study oneself for inner change, use out-of-body experiences for spiritual development, and how to progress spiritually. He was able to write concisely due to his firsthand experience of the process. <br /><br />His work covered a range of topics including OBEs, near-death experiences, dreams, self-discovery, spiritual exercises, esoteric knowledge and more. Belzebuub wrote that it is the development of consciousness that enables all spiritual experience.<br /><br />Belzebuub always gave his work freely as he believed a person’s finances shouldn’t be an obstacle to receiving spiritual knowledge. Belzebuub’s books were sold through an independent publisher with no royalties ever going to him. His websites, the courses he authored, and all his videos and audio, were always free to access.<br /><br />BELZEBUUB’S MEDIA APPEARANCES<br /><br />Belzebuub was interviewed between 1997 and 2009 as an expert on out-of-body experiences and dreams internationally on TV and radio, including on the BBC and Canadian daytime TV. Belzebuub also appeared on national TV as part of a panel of experts discussing the discovery of the ancient text the Gospel of Judas.</span>
