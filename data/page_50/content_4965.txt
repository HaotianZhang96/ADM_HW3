  <span id="freeText1501909411132489460" style="display:none">The Seduction of Genius !<br /><br />Generally , i'm not one for hyperbole and over egging the pudding but this book is like no other I've ever read . Every character that appears in it is examined in harsh detail with very few words and that's the key to this novel. It's short and pithy and appeals to a new digital generation managing to convey in only a very few sentences the wide cross section of the British and American mainstream. The sense of reality often indugled with exquisite detail of the surreal .The severe penetrating symbolism of class struggle and social divisons of society make it representational of a new style of writing and I think this is why Viola stands out as different . I hate to use the word edgy . It is richly decorated with allegory, style , and social satire . The story held together in a clearly defined classical narrative that cleverly brings the landscape of city life to the attention of the reader. Familiar sights and sounds: office people, clubs, resturants are greatly elaborated and given new depth providing a truthful and provacative account of urban struggles. From buildings ( law courts are seen in muted tones ) to grungy rock and roll haunts every aspect of life is captured with precision and artful grace. There is a real udnerstanding in this novel of the way rhythms , harmonies and symmetry of sound work together to develop a unique and balanced composition. That is why many readers point o the poetic association of the narrative , the elements are linked to art and music creating a visual and conceptual unity within the text . There is an almost endless and streaming play on words and the contrast of negative and positive.<br /><br />A perception of excess and hedonism seen through the main characters transfigures reality offering a springboard for more bohemian raphsody for the future . A subliminal read and hugely recommended . The fact that it is still largely unknown only adds to its allure . </span>
